---
title: "Buying"
description: "Buy skins from your CSBoard balance — opt-in and locked down."
---

Buying is opt-in and deliberately locked down.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable trading on your key">
    A normal key only reads. Flip on buying for your key in your
    [profile](https://csboard.com/profile?tab=api). Until then `POST /v1/orders`
    returns `403 trading_not_enabled` — a leaked read key can never spend.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link a Steam account + trade URL">
    Orders ship straight to Steam, so a linked account and trade URL are
    required, else `400 steam_account_required`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fund your balance">
    Buys debit your existing CSBoard balance. Top up on the site — there is no
    card/crypto over the API.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Overcharge protection

You are charged the **live price at execution**. `price_usd` from
[`/v1/listings`](/api-reference/get-listings) is authoritative (it equals the
charge); [`/v1/prices`](/api-reference/get-prices) is an indicative grouped
snapshot that can lag.

Always send `max_price_usd` — a total ceiling enforced atomically inside the
locked debit. If the live price moved past it, the order is rejected with
`409 price_moved` (and the current total), never overcharging you.

## Idempotency

Send an `Idempotency-Key` header (or `idempotency_key` in the body). A retried
request replays the original order instead of buying twice.

```bash
curl -X POST https://csboard.com/v1/orders \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer csb_pub_..." \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "Idempotency-Key: 6f9c2b10-1a2b-4c3d-8e4f-5a6b7c8d9e0f" \
  -d '{"item_ids":["ext_sp_8841201"],"max_price_usd":20.00}'
```

See [`POST /v1/orders`](/api-reference/post-orders) for the full reference.
